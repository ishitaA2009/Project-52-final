Camel

  1. Camels are social animals who roam the deserts in search of food and water
  2. There are two different types of camels: Dromedary Camels and Bractrion Camels.
  3. They are specially adapted to the life in desert.

Chameleon

  1. Chameleons are reptiles that are part of the iguana suborder
  2. Changing skin colour is an important part of the communication among chameleons
  3. Most chameleons have a prehensile tail that they use to wrap around tree branches

Panda

  1. There are two main types of pandas – the giant black-and-white panda and the Red Panda.
  2. The giant panda is native to China.
  3. The Giant panda is a bear and is the world’s most adored and protected rare animal.

Pigeon

  1. Pigeons are Very Fast.
  2. They are Incredibly Social.
  3. Pigeons are Spiritual to Some Religions.

Teddy-Bear

  1. The first toy bear is believed to be made by German toy maker Margarete Steiff in 1902
  2. Walt Disney produced the first colour cartoon film featuring teddy bears – Alice and the Three Bears – in 1924.
  3. The first British Teddy Bear Festival was held in 1989 in London.

Cicada

  1. Cicadas improve lawns by digging tunnels that allow air into the soil.
  2. They’re true bugs (from the order Hemiptera). So you can call them a bug and be scientifically accurate.
  3. The loud whirring or buzzing sound you hear is an all-male cicada chorus.
  4. Females make a much quieter sound by flicking their wings
  5. Most have red-orange eyes. But occasionally cicadas have blue, white or grayish eyes
