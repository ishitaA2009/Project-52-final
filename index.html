Camel

  1. Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms
  2. There are over 160 words for camel in Arabic alone.
  3. Mother camels carry their calves up to 14 months before giving birth.
  4. Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can’t eat.
  5. Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.

Chameleon

  1. Almost half of all known species live in Madagascar
  2. They change color in order to communicate, regulate body temperature and hide themselves from predators
  3. They have skin crystals which enable them to change color at will
  4. Their eyes can swivel around in two different directions simultaneously
  5. Smaller chameleons have faster tongues

Panda

  1. Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim.
  2. Pandas have so many fans because they look cute.
  3. Pandas are "lazy" — eating and sleeping make their day.
  4. An adult can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day
  5. Pandas have carnivorous teeth, but they eat bamboo and fruit.

Pigeon

  1. Pigeons can fly at altitudes up to and beyond 6000 feet, and at an average speed of 77.6 mph.
  2. They can detect sounds at far lower frequencies than humans are able to, and can thus hear distant storms and volcanoes.
  3. Pigeons are fed by many members of different religions including Muslims, Hindus and Sikhs for spiritual reasons.
  4. Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities.
  5. They can detect sounds at far lower frequencies than humans are able to, and can thus hear distant storms and volcanoes.

Teddy-Bear

  1. Teddy bears got their name from the story that Teddy Roosevelt refused to shoot a bear cub while on a hunting trip in 1902.
  2. The first toy stuffed bear was created by German toymaker Margarete Steiff. She created a soft plush with movable arms and legs and a friendly face after taking her nephew to the zoo in 1903.
  3. The world's smallest stitched teddy bear is a mere 0.29 inches tall! It was created by South African artist Cheryl Moss who is known for her minuscule work.
  4. The official term for a teddy bear collector is an ‘arctophile’.
  5. The Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906.

Cicada

  1. Cicadas improve lawns by digging tunnels that allow air into the soil.
  2. They’re true bugs (from the order Hemiptera). So you can call them a bug and be scientifically accurate.
  3. The loud whirring or buzzing sound you hear is an all-male cicada chorus.
  4. Females make a much quieter sound by flicking their wings
  5. Most have red-orange eyes. But occasionally cicadas have blue, white or grayish eyes
